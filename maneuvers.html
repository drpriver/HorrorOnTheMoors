Maneuvers

Initator level is like your caster level for maneuvers. Some maneuvers scale with initiator level. To take a maneuver, you need to meet its prerequisites and have an initator level as given by this table:

Maneuver Level Initiator Level
1 1
2 3
3 5

Barbarians, Fighters, Monks, Paladins and Rangers have initiator level equal to their class level. Bards and Rogues have initator level equal to one half their class level, rounded down, except for the Burglar's Tricks school. Clerics, Druids, Wizards and Sorcerers have initator level equal to 0. Multiclass characters use the better of their two classes.

A character can choose maneuvers as feats. Fighters and monks can choose maneuvers as their bonus feats.

You can also buy a stance with a feat. To buy a stance, you need to already have a maneuver of that school.

A character can only take maneuvers from 3 different schools.

Note that Diamond Mind has been removed due to its main focus not being in the game. The ones that remained were moved into other schools.

Burglar's tricks is a new school only for thieves.

Eagle Eye is a new school focused on ranged attacks.

Desert Wind and Shadow Hand are restricted to monks. In the future we might allow thief/mages to take them, tbd.

Some Devoted Spirit maneuvers are restricted to Paladins and Fighter/Cleric.

Setting Sun and Stone Dragon have been merged into Stone Dragon.

Maneuvers that target allies that don't specify you, don't affect you.

Bowling Ball

Bowling Ball

Stone Dragon (Strike)

Level: 3
Prerequisite: One Stone Dragon maneuver
Action: 1 standard action
Range: Unarmed Melee attack
Target: One creature
Special: May be used as part of a charge

To set up Bowling Ball, you must move at least 15 feet.

Make a single unarmed melee attack against a target. If your attack hits, you may make a bull rush check at a +4 bonus without the need to move into the target's space. If you win, the target is pushed back at least 10 feet without the need for you to follow. If your bull rush check is high, they may be pushed back more than 10 feet. You can't push them behind you, but you can push them in any direction in that 180° arc that is in front or to the side of you. The target falls prone and suffers 2d6 points of damage. If you shove them into any other creature, apply your same bull rush result to those he collides with. If it succeeds, they are knocked down and suffer 2d6 points of damage and the initial creature keeps moving. If it fails, the initial shoved creature stops moving.
